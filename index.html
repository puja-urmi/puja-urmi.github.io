About me

I am Puja Saha, an M.A.Sc. student in Computer Engineering at the University of Guelph, with a Bachelor's degree in Biomedical Engineering. My research focuses on privacy-preserving medical image analysis, decentralized machine learning, and multimodal AI for healthcare applications. I aim to develop robust, generalizable methods to address data disparities while ensuring high performance and data privacy in medical AI. I am actively seeking internship opportunities to apply my expertise to real-world challenges and contribute to advancing machine learning methodologies in impactful applications.

My Projects

  • Automatic Semantic Segmentation of Kidney and Tumor in CT scans: Developed a segmentation model for kidney and tumor regions in CT images, achieving notable performance on the KiTS19 dataset.
  • RAG-based Medical Chatbot: Designed a medical chatbot using Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) for answering health-related queries.
  • Human Pose Estimation: Worked on a deep learning model for real-time human pose estimation, applicable in healthcare, fitness, and animation.
  • Big Data-driven Job Market Analysis and Salary Prediction : Created a dynamic image enhancement framework that adapts based on the statistics of the dataset, used for segmentation and classification tasks in medical images.
